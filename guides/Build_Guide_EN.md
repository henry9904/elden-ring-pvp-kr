## 📖 What Is a Build?

A build is the combination of your **stats, armour, weapons, talismans, and any spells or incantations**. Planning your build in advance is strongly recommended for optimising both damage output and survivability.

## 📊 Core Stat Guidelines

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **60 Vigor** is always optimal in PvP (or 58 with Imp Head (Lion) helm).

### 🛡️ Poise Breakpoints

| Poise | Reason |
|:-----:|--------|
| **88** | Recommended baseline — use the armour optimiser in the build planner, targeting 88 poise **without** Bullgoat Talisman. Weapons like 2H Shamshir, Warped Axe, and Nagakiba become extremely oppressive if you cannot poise through their backswings. |
| **104** | Recommended against offhand Daggers — poises through the 2nd L1. |
| **105** | Required against offhand Thrusting Swords. |

> [!NOTE]
> A **backswing** refers to the second light attack (R1-2) of a weapon. It will hit you even if you reaction-roll the first R1, as long as you are in range. See the poise breakpoints sheet for full details.

## ❌ What NOT to Use

> [!CAUTION]
> **Never use a Soreseal or Scarseal talisman.** It is never worth it above approximately level ~80 (~45+ Vigor). The HP deficit outweighs the stat gains at competitive levels.

## 💎 Talismans

### Status Resistance

Adapting your talismans to counter your opponent's setup is often the deciding factor in a fight.

| Talisman | Resists |
|----------|---------|
| Stalwart Horn Charm +2 | Bleed |
| Immunizing Horn Charm +2 | Poison / Rot |
| Mottled Necklace +2 | Mixed status |
| Clarifying Horn Charm +2 | Madness / Sleep |
| Ailment Talisman | General status |

### Damage Reaction Talismans

If your opponent is exploiting large damage boosts, mirror them:

| Setup | Components |
|-------|-----------|
| Bleed reaction | Lord of Blood's Exultation + White Mask |
| Rot reaction | Kindred of Rot's Exultation + Mushroom Crown |

### Situational Talismans

| Talisman | Use Case |
|----------|----------|
| **Hammer Talisman** | Crucial vs shield users and shieldpokers — massively increases stamina damage to break guard |
| **Spear Talisman** | Significant damage boost to thrust counter hits (whiff punishes and trades) — recommended for thrust moveset weapons |
| **Millicent's Prosthesis / Rotten Winged Sword** | Multi-hit weapons (Twinblades, PS Straight Swords) |
| **Godskin Swaddling Cloth** | Multi-hit weapons — HP recovery on successive hits |

## 🔄 Hardswapping Talismans

Learning to hardswap talismans mid-fight when you take chip or significant damage is a **key skill** that can greatly decide duels.

**Recommended swap sequence:**

```
Start:   Ritual Shield/Sword  +  Crimson Amber Medallion +2  +  Erdtree's Favor +2
  ↓ take chip damage
Swap:    Blessed Dew Talisman
  ↓ low HP
Swap:    Blue Feathered Branchsword
```

> [!CAUTION]
> **Red Feathered Branchsword** is banned in most rulesets. In some rulesets it is only banned when paired with Blue Feather / Blessed Dew. Check your ruleset before using it.
>
> Scorpion Charms have niche uses — pairing with Red Feather for comebacks at very low HP, or against pure elemental builds (mages, Coded Sword, Cipher Pata). **Locking talismans** such as Great-Jar's Arsenal or Erdtree's Favor +2 are generally not ideal but sometimes necessary for specific builds.
