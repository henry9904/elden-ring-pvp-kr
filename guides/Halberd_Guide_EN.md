# 🪓 Elden Ring PvP — Halberd Guide

> [!NOTE]
> This guide covers Halberds in Elden Ring PvP, including moveset data, offhand options, and ash of war synergies.
> Original document by **eisenwave**. Markdown redesign by **Crong**.
> *Patch 1.07*

---

## 📊 Overview

Halberds are excellent **STR / DEX / ARC-scaling** weapons in PvP, solidly **A-tier** overall. Their strength lies in what is essentially the **best running attack in the game**, combined with offhand weapons, ashes of war, and follow-up attacks that produce a devastating vortex.

---

## 🔀 Moveset Types

Unlike other weapon classes, **the R1 attack differs significantly between individual halberds**, changing how each is played. Three distinct movesets exist:

| Moveset | Examples | Characteristics |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Vertical Slash** | Nightrider Glaive | Reactable, no pierce damage, acts as anti-air |
| **Thrusting** | Banished Knight's Halberd | Pierce damage on R1 = counter-damage on trades; R1 chain is slow and fully reactable |
| **Swordspear** | Guardian's Swordspear, Loretta's War Sickle | Extremely fast R1 follow-ups after RR1; 1H JR2 → R1 true combo; lower range |

> [!NOTE]
> **Swordspear** is arguably the best moveset — extremely fast follow-up R1s after RR1, excellent anti-air, and a 1H JR2 → R1 true combo (~1000 damage, ~1100 with Claw Talisman). Downside: comparably low range and no pierce damage on R1.
>
> **Thrusting** is second best — more pierce damage and counter-hits on trades/whiff-punishes, but slower R1 chain.
>
> **Vertical Slash** is the weakest — equally slow, no pierce damage. Only upside is anti-air.

## 🔍 Weapon Discussion

### 🥇 Guardian's Swordspear *(S-Tier)*

Arguably the best moveset with the highest physical damage (**757 AR at 73 DEX**). Benefits of the Swordspear moveset fully apply. Only downside: **considerably lower range** than other S-tier halberds.

---

### Nightrider Glaive *(S-Tier)*

Among the **highest damage and range** halberds. Very low DEX requirement — ideal for full STR builds. Downsides: one of the **heaviest halberds** and has the weak vertical slash moveset.

---

### Commander's Standard *(S-Tier)*

**Longest halberd**, slightly more damaging than Banished Knight's with the same moveset. Cannot be infused and has a nearly useless buffing weapon art (+2.5% damage). Still very useful as an **offhand weapon**.

---

### Banished Knight's Halberd *(S-Tier)*

Much less damage than Nightrider but with a **thrusting moveset** — extra counter-hits on R1 compensate for the damage gap. Much lighter than Nightrider, slightly shorter. Scales well with virtually any infusion; popular on FTH or INT hybrid builds.

---

### Ripple Crescent Halberd *(A-Tier)*

Special: **innate ARC scaling** boosts any applied status. Drawstring Sleep Grease on Ripple Crescent at high ARC procs sleep extremely fast. Downside: not infusible, stuck with Spinning Slash.

---

### Golden Halberd *(B-Tier)*

Highest AR of all halberds, but: vertical slash moveset, split damage, high STR requirement, high weight, not infusible. Only viable on high-level STR/FTH hybrid builds. **Rarely seen at RL 125.**

---

### Vulgar Militia Shotel *(C-Tier)*

Shorter than Banished Knight's with a worse moveset. Key feature: **40% of damage bypasses shields**. Cold Shotel can be strong against shield users.

---

### Vulgar Militia Saw *(C-Tier)*

Same range and moveset as Shotel, but with **innate 50 bleed buildup**. Boosted further by Seppuku or drawstring blood grease. Combining multiple saws with different status effects can be effective.

---

### Loretta's War Sickle *(D-Tier)*

Longer than Swordspear with the same moveset. Very high stat requirements and a poor weapon art. Viable at very high RL (e.g. RL 200) due to C scaling across three stats.

---

### Dragon Halberd *(D-Tier)*

A much shorter Banished Knight's Halberd. Weapon art buffs with 130 lightning + 80 frost — decent, but the short range holds it back.

---

### Gargoyle's Halberd *(E-Tier)*

Almost identical to Nightrider Glaive, but **dramatically shorter** — the shortest halberd in the game. Useless in all cases.

---

### Gargoyle's Black Halberd *(F-Tier)*

High stat requirements, split damage, stuck with Spinning Slash, and short range. No redeeming qualities.

---

## ⚔️ Moveset Data

*Data references Banished Knight's Halberd. Other halberds are very similar except R1/R2.*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Regardless of 1H or 2H, halberds have an **excellent, extremely fast, very low recovery RR1 with pierce damage**. With Spear Talisman, expect to deal approximately your physical AR as damage on a counter-hit (e.g. 700 AR Keen Swordspear ≈ 700 damage on counter-hit). After landing RR1, you can follow up with weapon arts, offhand attacks, R1, or another RR1 — all while the opponent is staggered — creating a devastating vortex.

### Two-Handed

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|
| R1 | 101.5 | 103 |
| R2 (no charge) | 110 | 130 |
| R2 (full charge) | 55 + 275 | 50 + 150 |
| RR1 | 101.5 | 110 |
| RR2 | 110 | 125 |
| CR1 | 101.5 | 95 |
| JR1 | 101.5 | 110 |
| JR2 | 220 | 135 |
| BR1 | 101.5 | 95 |
| Guard Counter | 330 | 130 |

In ladder matches restricted to **100 poise**, 2H breaks poise on every single attack — a significant advantage when opponents run 99 poise builds.

2H RR1 has slightly less range than 1H RR1. On Swordspear specifically: 2H RR1 doesn't combo into R1 2 (slightly slower follow-up), and 2H JR2 → R1 is **not** a true combo — making 2H considerably worse than 1H for Swordspear. For thrusting halberds, 2H is slightly better (faster CR1, faster RR1 → R1, faster JR2 → R1).

### One-Handed ⭐ *(Recommended)*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|
| R1 | 92.5 | 100 |
| R2 (no charge) | 100 | 125 |
| R2 (full charge) | 300 | 160 |
| RR1 | 92.5 | 105 |
| RR2 | 100 | 120 |
| CR1 | 92.5 | 90 |
| JR1 | 92.5 | 107 |
| JR2 | 200 | 130 |
| BR1 | 92.5 | 90 |

1H is generally preferred — never much worse than 2H, and grants access to **offhand weapons**, which synergize extremely well. BR1 is very fast and long-range with pierce damage, especially useful on non-thrusting halberds.

The **Swordspear 1H RR1 → R1** combo is extremely fast (uses R1 2), barely reactable, and fast enough to interrupt Raptor of the Mists activated on the RR1.

### Powerstanced

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|
| L1 | 55.5 + 55.5 | 92 + 92 |
| RL1 | 55.5 + 55.5 | 100 + 100 |
| CL1 | 55.5 + 55.5 | 85 + 85 |
| JL1 | 75 + 75 | 115 + 115 |
| BL1 | 55.5 + 55.5 | 85 + 85 |

Any paired attack deals **over 100 poise damage** — excellent against high-poise opponents. JL1 easily achieves **~1000 damage with Claw Talisman**, with both halberds swinging vertically downward — devastating in air-to-air and air-to-ground combat alike.

> [!NOTE]
> L1 is slow with two separate hits — useful for roll-catching but risky otherwise. CL1 hits hard when it lands fully, but tracking is extremely bad and half-hits are common. RL1 can roll-catch opponents who roll through the first swing with suboptimal timing. BL1 is slow and low-damage.

## 🤚 Offhand Weapon Options

### Thrusting Sword (Offstoc) — *Most Traditional* ⭐

| Weapon | Offhand L1 Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|--------|:--------------------:|:---------:|
| Estoc | 40 | 100 |

After landing RR1, Offstoc L1 is a near-true-combo — very fast and barely reactable. Forces the opponent to choose between eating it or panic-rolling, which you then roll-catch with halberd attacks.

Offstoc is also essential for **whiff-punishing and roll-catching** on reaction — something halberds are often too slow to do alone.

> [!CAUTION]
> Offstoc is very easily jumpable. It also deals low poise damage — requires 3 hits to break 100 poise, and loses most trades without a counter-hit.

---

### Nagakiba (Offkiba)

| Weapon | Offhand L1 Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|--------|:--------------------:|:---------:|
| Nagakiba | 50 | 100 |

Diagonal swings offer **anti-air capability** and similar range to Cleanrot Knight's Offstoc. Tremendously better than Offstoc against **poise-restricted setups** (UGS, PS GS at 49 poise) — those setups poise Offstoc, but will always be staggered by Offkiba.

---

### Straight Sword (Offsword)

| Weapon | Offhand L1 Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|--------|:--------------------:|:---------:|
| Broadsword | 50 | 100 |

Similar to Offkiba but less vertical range and much lower total range. Prefer Offkiba if stat requirements are met. Offhand Coded Sword is a good emergency option for FTH casters who've run out of FP.

---

### Pike (Offpike)

| Weapon | Offhand L1 Poise Dmg | Attack MV |
|--------|:--------------------:|:---------:|
| Pike | 50 | 100 |

Decent offhand even when the opponent can poise it. Most useful for **Guardian's Swordspear** — whose very low-range R1 struggles to whiff-punish or catch rolls at distance. Offpike fills that gap at huge range.

---

### Sacred Seal

| Spell | PvP Poise Dmg | Stamina Dmg |
|-------|:-------------:|:-----------:|
| Catch Flame | 70 | 30 |
| Bestial Sling | 50 | 40 |

Switch between **Bestial Sling** (far) and **Catch Flame** (close) depending on distance. Catch Flame integrates into the halberd vortex and deals ~450 per hit. Against PS SS (65 poise cap) and UGS/PS GS (49 poise cap), these spells always break poise.

## 🔥 Ashes of War

> [!CAUTION]
> **Under the current ladder ruleset, Flaming Strike, Storm Stomp, and Glintstone Pebble are banned on halberds.**

### Meta Ashes

Halberds synergize amazingly with meta ashes — RR1 creates long enough stagger with short enough recovery to create roll-catching combos.

| Ash | Use |
|-----|-----|
| **Glintstone Pebble** | Roll-catches after RR1 when timed correctly. On a magic halberd, nearly one-shots any opponent. *(Banned)* |
| **Flaming Strike** | Roll-catch combo after RR1 for massive damage. Solves the issue of opponents jumping into thrusting/vertical halberds. *(Banned)* |
| **Storm Stomp** | Roll-catches after RR1. Best on thrusting halberds — reaches regardless of how far the opponent was staggered. *(Banned)* |
| **Quickstep** | Weak on halberds — doesn't enhance the vortex and halberd rolling attacks aren't strong. Mostly pointless. |

---

### Melee Ashes

#### 🌀 Spinning Slash — *Anti-parry and Roll-catch*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Spinning Slash 1 | 62.5 | 105 | 100 | 125 |
| Spinning Slash 2 | 37.5 + 62.5 | 60 + 110 | 100 + 100 | 75 + 125 |

Halberds get a **slow version** — less damage and slightly slower than the fast variant. Main use: punishing opponents who roll into you or attempt backstabs (hits everywhere around you). Also unparryable — good against shields and parry attempts. RR1 → Double Slash is a roll-catching combo (second hit of L2 connects).

---

#### ✂️ Double Slash — *Decent Roll-catch*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Double Slash 1 | 37.5 + 50 | 92 + 105 | 75 + 100 | 75 + 75 |
| Double Slash 2 | 37.5 + 37.5 + 50 | 84 + 86 + 115 | 75 + 75 + 100 | 75 + 75 + 100 |
| Double Slash 3 | 100 | 145 | 100 | 150 |

Identical to the fast version in damage, status, stamina, and poise damage — just slower. Useful for catching opponents dancing around you with quickstep. Not a poise break in ladder matches (even the final hit). Theoretically very parryable, but opponents rarely attempt this in practice.

---

#### 💃 Sword Dance — *Okay on Halberds*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Sword Dance 1 | 65 + 65 | 130 + 130 | 75 + 100 | 130 + 130 |
| Sword Dance 2 | 75 | 150 | 100 | 150 |

The slow variant on halberds actually deals **more poise damage and damage** than the fast version. High range halberds make it reach from very far. Provides anti-air capability for thrusting halberds. Best against poise-restricted setups where one half of L2 1 breaks poise (PS SS, UGS, Moonveil, PS GS). Can be strafed, react-parried, and backstabbed.

---

#### 🎯 Impaling Thrust — *High Value*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Impaling Thrust | 300 | 170 | 100 | 200 |

**RR1 → Impaling Thrust is a roll-catching combo** (1H and 2H) — works only if the opponent panic-rolls roughly straight away from you. Good tracking, but diagonal rolls toward you will avoid it.

Condition opponents into panic-rolling (e.g. use Offstoc after RR1 first), then land Impaling Thrust on the second RR1. Also **ignores 80% of shields** — devastating against blocking opponents. A counter-hit with Spear Talisman can deal **~1400 damage**.

> [!NOTE]
> Essentially a much better charged R2 — similar speed, more damage. You will rarely find opportunities to use it, but when it lands, it's huge.

---

#### 🔵 Glintblade Phalanx — *Overpowered in Theory*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Stamina Dmg |
|------|:-------------:|:-----------:|
| Each Phalanx Blade | 100 | 25 |

Four synergies with RR1: Phalanx staggers → RR1 connects; opponent rolls RR1 → caught by Phalanx; opponent rolls Phalanx → caught by RR1; RR1 staggers → Phalanx hits during stagger. When played optimally, **theoretically impossible to avoid** the combination. One of the best ashes for halberds.

---

#### 🔥 Flame of the Redmanes — *Discount Flaming Strike*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Stamina Dmg |
|------|:-------------:|:-----------:|
| Flame of the Redmanes | 400 | 100 |

With Flaming Strike banned, this is the closest alternative. Very slow and reactable — most hits come from opponent mistakes. Can combo into RR1 if the opponent is late-staggered. Deals upward of 600 damage on high STR builds.

---

#### ❄️ Ice Spear — *Powerful Defense*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | St. Dmg MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Startup Spin | 100 | 80 | 100 | 80 |
| Projectile | 250 | N/A | N/A | 150 |

The startup spin is **barely reactable**, spawns a large hitbox, deals 100 poise damage, and combos into the projectile if the opponent is staggered (~1000 damage on an optimized build). Excellent punishment for opponents who walk close or roll into you — especially common against 2H halberds.

Downside: very short range, mostly a defensive tool. The projectile can be strafed.

---

#### 🌫️ Chilling / Poisonous Mist — *Status Pressure*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Chilling Mist | 100 | 170 | 100 | 200 |
| Poisonous Mist | 100 | 170 | 100 | 200 |

At high latency, phantom hits on RR1 become nearly unavoidable — making status application from spammed running attacks very reliable. Chilling Mist (cold) and Poisonous Mist (poison) can be placed on cold/poison halberds for amplified buildup. A Poison Halberd with Poisonous Mist at 10 ARC can proc poison in just **two attacks**.

## 📋 Summary

| Playstyle | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary** | 1H halberd + offhand weapon (Offstoc / Offkiba) |
| **vs. 92+ poise** | Switch to 2H for guaranteed poise breaks |
| **vs. High poise (PS)** | Powerstanced halberds — any L1 deals 100+ poise damage |
| **Best overall setup** | Guardian's Swordspear 1H + Offstoc or Offpike |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The halberd vortex is built around **RR1** → follow-up. Master the transition from RR1 into ashes of war, offhand attacks, and R1 based on how the opponent responds to the stagger.
