## 📊 Overview

Katanas are viable **DEX-scaling** weapons in PvP, solidly **A-tier** overall. Their key strengths lie in a **very diverse moveset** and **excellent synergy with ashes of war**. They are arguably the most ash-dependent weapons in the game — and also the weapons that synergize best with ashes.

## 🔍 Weapon Discussion

### 🥇 Nagakiba *(S-Tier)*

Infusible with by far the **greatest hitbox range of all katanas**. Scales with DEX — typically best with lightning infusion. The range advantage is so massive that only the Nagakiba is worth seriously using. Other katanas (e.g. Uchigatana on DEX) may have slightly higher AR and lower stat requirements, but the massive range disadvantage makes them non-viable.

All relevant ashes — Spinning Slash, Double Slash, Unsheathe — become significantly more powerful on Nagakiba due to its reach.

---

### 🥈 Moonveil *(A-Tier)*

Only A-tier because of its **extremely powerful weapon art**. Low range compared to Nagakiba, doesn't compensate with high AR. Effectively a weapon-art-delivery vehicle.

---

### Hand of Malenia *(B-Tier)*

Similar range to Nagakiba, more damage than Keen Nagakiba, 50 blood loss. Cannot be infused — stuck with Waterfowl Dance (poor in PvP). Decent offhand option. Lightning infusion is generally preferred over physical for katanas since it's not affected by Boiled Crab / Black Flame's Protection and lightning defence is only 127 at RL 125.

---

### Uchigatana *(C-Tier)*

A much shorter Nagakiba with lower stat requirements and slightly higher AR. The massive range disadvantage makes those advantages meaningless. At least it's infusible, giving access to strong ashes.

---

### Rivers of Blood *(C-Tier)*

Used to be A-tier. Patch 1.06 significantly nerfed Corpse Piler's bloody slashes — much weaker and no longer a major PvP threat.

---

### Serpentbone Blade *(C-Tier)*

Longer than Uchigatana, more damage than Keen Nagakiba, comes with Double Slash, and builds up **66 deadly poison per hit**. Unique R2 functions as a shorter-range Double Slash with more poise damage.

**Poison comparison:**

| Poison Type | Dmg/Tick (2000 HP) | Duration | Total Dmg |
|-------------|:-----------------:|:--------:|:---------:|
| Poison 1 (infusion) | 8 | 90 s | 720 |
| Poison 2 (Serpentbone) | 16 | 30 s | 480 |
| Scarlet Rot 1 (rot grease) | 18 | 90 s | 1620 |

---

### Dragonscale Blade *(D-Tier)*

Shorter than Uchigatana with less AR than an infused Uchigatana. Only notable feature: unique weapon art buffing with **80 frost buildup and 130 damage** — makes it an okay offhand option. Only katana without innate blood loss.

---

### Meteoric Ore Blade *(E-Tier)*

Simultaneously the **shortest and heaviest katana**. Nothing to offer over a Magic Uchigatana with Gravitas, which would also have lower stat requirements.

The PS moveset unlocks the two outstanding moves that define katana's CQC game:

#### 🔑 CL1

Hits with **both katanas simultaneously**, good range on Nagakiba. Compensates for the otherwise slow and poorly tracking L1. Can be extremely strong while wavedashing.

#### 🔑 JL1 *(Outstanding)*

Very high range, very high damage. Hits comparably early (katanas thrust downward). Not ideal for air-to-air, but **extremely strong as a forward-jump and neutral-jump for self-defence**. Expect **~850 damage with Claw Talisman** on Lightning Nagakibas.

> [!NOTE]
> L1 is slow, doesn't deal much poise damage, and doesn't use full Nagakiba range except on L1 3. Risky unless you have initiative. RL1 only good for chasing opponents rolling backward (both attacks have poor tracking).

## 🔥 Ashes of War

### Meta Ashes

Katanas don't have great synergy with meta ashes. They work best with **lightning infusion and melee ashes**.

| Ash | Assessment |
|-----|-----------|
| **Flaming Strike** | Useful roll-catch after JL1 on STR katanas — but opponents rarely roll into katanas, limiting value. |
| **Storm Stomp** | Overall bad — only gets ½ L1 or R1 after stomp (unless opponent has ≤20 poise). Spinning Slash is a stronger defensive option. |
| **Quickstep** | Gives convenient CL1 access, but crouching already does that. Only useful against specific setups (e.g. escaping true combos — recovery into QS is lower than into roll). |

---

### Melee Ashes

Due to the low poise damage of L1 and R1, and the overall weakness of regular L1, **ashes of war are critical to katana success**. Nagakiba's massive range amplifies every melee ash beyond what's possible on other weapons.

---

#### 🌀 Spinning Slash — *Braindead Powerful*

*Expect ~800 damage if all three hits connect.*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Spinning Slash 1 | 145 | 110 | 100 | 150 |
| Spinning Slash 2 | 45 + 145 | 65 + 115 | 100 + 100 | 85 + 150 |

A **death tornado** on Nagakiba. Massive range, first and last hit are poise breaks against any opponent. Completely unparryable, deals high stamina damage (can break medium shields in one spin), and excellent for applying status effects (e.g. Drawstring Blood Grease → bloody spin on top of innate bleed).

> [!NOTE]
> **Prefer Keen Nagakiba with Drawstring Blood Grease over Blood Nagakiba.**

**Spinning Slash True Combos:** L2 1 → L2 2 (first hit) is a true combo. If the opponent is poise-broken by the first part of L2 2 (45 poise damage), the final hit also connects. Even if they roll the last hit, a phantom-hit is extremely likely — Cold Nagakiba applies 315 frost, Poison Nagakiba applies 285 poison per spin.

> [!CAUTION]
> Loses if the opponent can interrupt by trading into Spinning Slash (first part alone deals low damage). Can also be countered by Raptor of the Mists (even if the first part of L2 2 hits them mid-air, it usually won't interrupt the jump attack).

#### ⚔️ Unsheathe — *Powerful Allrounder* ⭐

*Expect ~500 damage on L2 R1 / ~750 damage on L2 R2.*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|
| Unsheathe R1 | 150 | 190 | 100 | 200 |
| Unsheathe R2 | 300 | 245 | 100 | 300 |

Massive poise damage and damage in a comparably fast attack. L2 R2 poise damage is so high it can interrupt Storm Stomp.

| Move | Best Use |
|------|----------|
| **L2 R1** | Opponents rolling sideways or into you (better tracking) |
| **L2 R2** | Stationary targets; **best anti-air move in the game**; likely to break small/medium shields |

**Ways to land Unsheathe despite the pre-telegraph:**
- Read the opponent and trade or interrupt their attack
- Sheathe close to them — either react-trade or roll-catch
- Whiff-punish or trade into slow ashes (e.g. Waves of Darkness, Golden Land, Chilling Mist — L2 R2 wins even in a trade)

**Unsheathe Vortex:** Cancel recovery by crouching → hold L2 to re-sheathe immediately → Unsheathe at high speed. Unsheathe → Unsheathe is a roll-catching combo. At this speed, opponents who roll immediately may get caught by L2; those who don't get hit by a fast, high-damage attack.

---

#### 🎯 Impaling Thrust / Piercing Fang — *Major Punishes*

| Move | PvP Poise Dmg | Attack MV | Status MV | Stamina MV | Shield Chip |
|------|:-------------:|:---------:|:---------:|:----------:|:-----------:|
| Impaling Thrust | 300 | 187 | 100 | 200 | 85% |
| Piercing Fang | 300 | 212 | 100 | 200 | 100% |

Both became much more attractive in 1.07 (increased speed, poise damage, damage relative to Unsheathe). No known roll-catching combos yet, but both are excellent punishes for whiffed ashes, charged R2s, jump attacks, and similar slow/high-recovery moves. Pierce damage provides significant bonus on counter-hits.

## ⚔️ Matchups

| Result | Matchup | Notes |
|:------:|---------|-------|
| ✅ Soft counter | **Heavy Thrusting Sword** | CL1 dodges HTS R1 and RR2 (both go overhead); crouching also ducks these. Unsheathe wins trades and knocks HTS out of air; can also win vs. Storm Stomp and punish Flaming Strike. HTS has no answer for JL1. |
| ⚖️ Even | **PS Straight Swords** | Passive play with CL1 whiff-punishes can frustrate PS SS. Spinning Slash is strong here but countered by Raptor of the Mists. PS SS JL1 interrupts Spinning Slash — hard to react-punish with Unsheathe. Spinning Slash is excellent vs. empty jumps, whiffed jumps, and jumps that overshoot. |
| ⚠️ Soft countered | **2H Halberd** | Everything 2H halberd does breaks poise — Unsheathe gets interrupted, Spinning Slash and Double Slash become nearly useless. Only JL1 remains viable for self-defence against halberd RR1. |
| ⚠️ Soft countered | **UGS (vs. PS Kibas)** | Same as 2H halberd — all UGS attacks break poise. Everything except JL1 gets outtraded and outranged. Higher latency worsens the matchup significantly (jumping becomes less reliable). |

## 📋 Summary

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Katanas are the **most ash-dependent** weapon class in the game. Picking the right ash is critical to success.
>
> Default setup: **Lightning Nagakiba 1H or PS** + Spinning Slash or Unsheathe.
>
> Use **CL1 and JL1** as your primary PS damage tools — they define what makes katanas competitive at high level.

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary setup** | PS Lightning Nagakibas + Spinning Slash |
| **Against high-poise** | Switch to Unsheathe for massive poise damage |
| **Against low-poise** | 1H katana with JR2 and RR2 is sufficient |
| **High latency** | Spinning Slash phantom hits apply enormous status buildup |
