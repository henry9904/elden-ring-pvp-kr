## 📊 Overview

TB is all about **aggression and pressure**. Setting up poise-chips and knowing when you have priority is key with this weapon.

> [!CAUTION]
> Poise chips are **ineffective against hyperarmour** — every hyperarmour attack from an opponent resets their poise. Adjust your approach accordingly.

---

## 🥊 Moveset Breakdown

### 🦘 Empty Jumps

Used to apply pressure. If the opponent is familiar with TB then they likely know how difficult it can be to avoid the JR1 — it has a pretty substantial amount of phantom range that is only increased with higher latency.

While it can be a big risk to empty jump — since the opponent can attack to anti-air you or trade (especially with hyperarmour weapons) — it has the potential to put the opponent on the defensive, make them give up position, or even force a panic roll.

---

### 🔑 JR1 — Bread and Butter

**The core of TB.** Depending on how the opponent is oriented to you and on whether or not they are already chipped, the JR1 can absolutely change a fight in your favor and/or set you up for a RR1, CR1, or RR2.

The JR1 is a **two-stage attack** — an upwards swing followed by a downwards swing.

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Upward hit lands, opponent **does not break** | They were already chipped |
| Upward hit lands, opponent **breaks** | Downward swing lands before they can recover — confirmed combo |

> [!TIP]
> **If in doubt, JR1 out.**

---

### 🔨 JR2 — The Hammer

- If you **think** you can jump out of hitstun without being punished
- Or if you **think** your opponent is going to initiate an attack you know can be jumped

**→ Just JR2 their ass.**

Good examples: Great Katana, Great Spears, UGS poke, Haldag or Halstoc, Claymore, PSGS.

> [!WARNING]
> Halaxe will **always** anti-air JR2. Do not attempt it.

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Opponent likely to RR1 from halberd | Jump on prediction → JR2 |
| Getting vortexed by PSGS, they go for CR1 roll catch | Do **not** roll → JR2 |

> [!NOTE]
> **Best case:** lands for ~800 damage.
> **Worst case:** they roll and you reset to neutral.

The JR2 also has **excellent tracking** and is near impossible to side-strafe.

---

### 🔁 RR1 — The Chipper

Your **chip damage tool**. It is fast and hard to reaction-roll, and even if they do roll, it is very likely they still walk away chipped.

RR1 is essentially a **setup tool** — designed to poise-chip your opponent down and set them up for a guaranteed poise break with RR2.

> [!TIP]
> RR1 has **native anti-air**. Use it in place of RR2 if your opponent tends to jump frequently.

---

### ⚔️ RR2 — The Blender

**The decapitator. The fight ender.** RR2 is your primary offensive attack with TB.

If they are poise-chipped and you see an opening — **use it.** After landing this, you can go for a roll catch (or punish hyperarmour mash) with **Spinning Slash** ash of war. In an unrestricted environment, Flaming Strike serves the same role.

> [!NOTE]
> Personal opinion: the **horizontal RR2 on the KTS-Twinblade** is the best version of this move.

RR2 is also **incredible at roll catching.**

---

### 🦵 CR1

The best neutral attack for whiff punishes — it's very fast, has low recovery, respectable damage, and even a half hit or phantom hit will poise chip the opponent. Strong wavedash pressure after poise-chip as well.

It can land quite consistently after a RR2.

> [!TIP]
> CR1 has **native anti-air** as well.

---

### 👊 R1 — The Mash

Strong mash option against setups that have weak priority. However, against **111 poise** — and especially hyperarmour setups — use **Spinning Slash** first to condition them against mashing back before going into R1.

If your opponent makes the silly choice to roll into you during your mash, you've pretty much already won due to the amount of damage they're going to eat.

Due to the **speed and low recovery** of the R1 chain, you can also use it once and typically roll out before getting punished.

---

### 🔙 Backstep & Reverse Backstep

**Insanely good. Do not sleep on it.**

- RR2 into backstep R1 is one of the best setups for a **roll catch** — though be aware the opponent is often incentivised to mash out of hitstun, so the risk is real
- Also a great option to throw your opponent off balance and continue applying pressure

---

### ⚡ Neutral R2 & Charged R2

> [!CAUTION]
> **Avoid at all costs.** You can throw it out occasionally for the memes, but it will get you punished more often than not.

**Exception:** a fully charged R2 used on an opponent during wake-up (e.g., during recovery frames from a backstab) lands fairly consistently and can be used as a **knowledge check**.

If it lands — congrats, you just won the fight.

---

## 📋 Summary

| Move | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Empty Jump** | Apply pressure, force defensive reactions |
| **JR1** | Bread and butter, chip and confirm |
| **JR2** | Punish predictable attacks, anti-vortex |
| **RR1** | Chip damage, anti-air, poise break setup |
| **RR2** | Primary damage, fight ender, roll catch |
| **CR1** | Force rolls, whiff punish, anti-air follow-up |
| **R1** | Pressure mash, punish poor positioning |
| **Backstep R1** | Roll catch setup, pressure continuation |
| **Charged R2** | Wake-up knowledge check (niche) |
