## 📌 Overview

TB is all about **aggression and pressure**. Trust your prio and believe in the power of your mash.

---

## 🥊 Move Breakdown

### Empty Jumps

Used to apply pressure. If the opponent is familiar with TB then they likely know how difficult it can be to avoid the JR1 — it has a pretty substantial amount of phantom range that is only increased with higher latency.

Anyways, it's never a bad idea to just **empty jump when closing range** since it has the potential to put the opponent on the defensive and give up position, or even force a panic roll.

---

### JR1 — Bread and Butter

**The core of TB.** Depending on how the opponent is oriented to you and on whether or not they're already chipped, the JR1 can absolutely change a fight in your favor and/or set you up for a RR1/RR2.

The JR1 is a **two-stage attack** — an upwards swing followed by a downwards swing.

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Upward hit lands, opponent **doesn't break** | They were already chipped |
| Upward hit lands, opponent **breaks** | Downward swing lands before they can recover — confirmed combo |

> [!TIP]
> **If in doubt, JR1 out.**

---

### JR2 — The Hammer

I could talk about JR2 at length, but in summary:

- If you **think** you can jump out of hitstun without being punished
- Or if you **think** your opponent is going to initiate an attack you know can be jumped

**→ JR2 their ass.**

Good examples: Hal-axe, Claymore, PSGS.

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Opponent likely to RR1 from halberd | Jump it on prediction → JR2 |
| Getting vortexed by PSGS, they're going for CR1 roll catch | Don't roll → JR2 |

> [!NOTE]
> **Best case:** lands for \~800 damage.
> **Worst case:** they roll and you reset to neutral.

The JR2 also has **excellent tracking** and is near impossible to side-strafe.

---

### RR1 — The Chipper

Your **chip damage tool**. It's fast and hard to reaction-roll, and even if they do roll, it's very likely they'll still walk away chipped.

RR1 is essentially a **setup tool** — designed to chip your opponent down and set them up for a guaranteed poise break with RR2.

> [!TIP]
> RR1 has **native anti-air**. Use it in place of RR2 if your opponent tends to jump frequently.

---

### RR2 — The Blender

**The decapitator. The fight ender.** RR2 is your primary offensive attack with TB.

If they're chipped and you see an opening — **throw it.**

> [!NOTE]
> Personal opinion: the **horizontal RR2 on the KTS-Twinblade** is the best version of this move.

RR2 is also **incredible at roll catching.**

---

### CR1

Good attack to help **force a roll out**. I honestly don't use it as often as I should due to running sub-optimal poise. It can land quite consistently after a RR2.

> [!TIP]
> CR1 has **native anti-air** as well.

---

### R1 — The Mash

**Mash that shit and don't stop.**

If your opponent makes the silly choice to roll into you during your mash, you've pretty much already won due to the amount of damage they're going to eat.

Due to the **speed and low recovery** of the R1 chain, you can also mash and typically roll out before getting punished.

---

### Backstep & Reverse Backstep

**Insanely good. Don't sleep on it.**

- RR2 into backstep R1 is one of the best setups for a roll catch
- Also a great option to throw your opponent off balance and continue applying pressure

---

### Neutral R2 & Charged R2

> [!CAUTION]
> **Avoid at all costs.** You can throw it out occasionally for the memes, but it'll get you punished more often than not.

**Exception:** A fully charged R2 used on an opponent during wake-up (e.g., recovery frames from a backstab) lands fairly consistently and can be used as a **knowledge check**.

If it lands — congrats, you just won the fight.

## ✅ Summary

| Move | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Empty Jump** | Apply pressure, force defensive reactions |
| **JR1** | Bread and butter, chip and confirm |
| **JR2** | Punish predictable attacks, anti-vortex |
| **RR1** | Chip damage, anti-air, poise break setup |
| **RR2** | Primary damage, fight ender, roll catch |
| **CR1** | Force rolls, anti-air follow-up |
| **R1** | Pressure mash, punish poor positioning |
| **Backstep R1** | Roll catch setup, pressure continuation |
| **Charged R2** | Wake-up knowledge check (niche) |
