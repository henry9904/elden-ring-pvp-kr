## A

### 🎮 Aggressive / Passive — Reactive / Read-Heavy

Players are often categorised along two axes, forming a **quadrant playstyle graph**:

| Axis | Option A | Option B |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Timing** | **Reactive** — waits for the opponent to act, then responds | **Read-heavy** — predicts the opponent's action and pre-commits |
| **Distance** | **Passive** — holds space, waits for the opponent to approach | **Aggressive** — actively closes distance |

Players are sometimes referred to by their quadrant position (e.g. *"yellow quadrant player"*, *"blue quadrant player"*).

---

### 🎯 Aimpunch

When a player is hit during an attack — even if they poise through it — a **flinch animation blends** into their current attack animation. This can redirect the weapon's hitbox or cause it to miss entirely.

> **See also:** [Why Do Elden Ring Hitboxes Randomly Miss](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x_KwMEwYxHM) (YouTube)

---

## B

### ↩️ Backswing

The second hit in a light attack chain (R1 → R1 2). Many movesets swing forward on the first R1, then back the opposite direction on the second.

> [!NOTE]
> When an attack **"has backswing"**, it means the second hit places the opponent into **frame disadvantage** — their hitstun lasts longer than the attacker's recovery into the follow-up.

---

### 🛡️ Block Cancel

A catch-all term for using a brief guard input to cancel unwanted animations:

- Cancelling a running pivot to maintain momentum
- Cancelling the walking state to repeat running jumps
- Running jumps toward the opponent → instantly run back without pivoting
- Cancelling attack recovery *(see Recovery Cancel)*
- Cancelling the crouch state (e.g. to perform a neutral R1)

> **See also:** [Elden Ring Tech: Block Canceling](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zYVuEd2Pr2c) (YouTube)

---

## C

### 💢 Chip Damage

Small amounts of damage (typically **below 10% of player HP**) from consumables, projectiles, or certain melee attacks that bypass blocking partially.

---

### ⚡ Counter-Hit / Counter-Damage

Attacks have **counter-frames** during and shortly after their active frames. Getting hit during these frames reduces the defender's pierce damage negation — this is a **counter-hit**.

**Spear Talisman** adds +15% pierce damage on a counter-hit, for a combined ~**50% extra pierce damage**. A white spark near the blood splatter indicates counter-damage has occurred.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Counter-hits only affect **pierce damage**. Affinity components (e.g. lightning on a thrusting weapon) are **not** increased. This is why pure physical thrusting weapons are generally preferred in competitive PvP.

Some attacks look like thrusts but deal no pierce damage *(e.g. Serpent Hunter crouch poke)*, and some non-thrust attacks do deal pierce damage *(e.g. Serpent Hunter JR2)*.

---

### ⚔️ Critical Attack — Backstab / Riposte

After **breaking an opponent's guard** or **successfully parrying**, your next R1 becomes a powerful **Riposte**. Performed close behind an opponent, it becomes a **Backstab**.

Both grant i-frames to both players during the animation and deal massive damage.

## D

### 🔢 Damage Count

A **hidden hit counter** (also in Dark Souls 3). Each hit increases the count. Rolling, attacking, or exiting hitstun resets it to zero.

> [!NOTE]
> After the **4th consecutive hit**, you can instantly roll out of any hitstun. However, the window to use a weapon art is **never shortened** by damage count.

> **See also:** [[DS3] The Hidden Mechanic Behind Combos | DamageCount](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hkgpFB4OS1E) (YouTube)

### 🔄 Dead Angling

In older Soulsborne titles, manually angling attacks sideways bypasses a blocker's shield (which only covers the front). In Elden Ring, the term is **loosely used** for any manually aimed attack technique. See also: *Free-Aiming*.

---

### 🧮 Defence and Damage Negation

Two separate systems reduce incoming damage:

| System | Source |
|--------|--------|
| **Defence** | Scales from player stats |
| **Damage Negation (Absorption)** | Comes from armour and buffs |

**Damage formula:**
```python
def damage(atk, defence, abs):
    result = atk * (1 - abs / 100)
    if defence > 8.0 * atk:    return result * 0.10
    if defence > 1.0 * atk:    return result * (19.2/49 * (atk/defence - 0.125)**2 + 0.1)
    if defence > 0.4 * atk:    return result * (-0.4/3  * (atk/defence - 2.5)**2   + 0.7)
    if defence > 0.125 * atk:  return result * (-0.8/121* (atk/defence - 8)**2     + 0.9)
    else:                      return result * 0.90
```

The curve produces **increasing returns** — the higher your AR already is relative to the opponent's defence, the more each additional point of damage matters. Damage negation acts as a simple final multiplier.

> [!NOTE]
> **Competitive Defence** ≈ 140 defence / 30% negation — a solid benchmark at RL 125 with good armour. Lightning defence is typically lower (~127 at RL 125); fire defence is usually higher (scales with Vigor).

## E

### 🦘 Empty Jump

Jumping toward an opponent **without attacking**. Used to bait panic rolls — opponents typically don't want to trade with a jump attack, so they often dodge, which can then be punished on landing.

## F

### ⚡ Fast Weapon Art vs. Slow Weapon Art

**Fast WAs** can be activated on the **1st frame** after an attack's active frames end — essentially zero recovery. **Slow WAs** require **4 frames** of waiting first.

**Fast weapon arts include** *(non-exhaustive)*: No Skill, Lion's Claw, Double Slash, Unsheathe, Square Off, Lightning Ram, Flame of the Redmanes, Determination, Royal Knight's Resolve, Quickstep, Bloodhound's Step, Raptor of the Mists, Impaling Thrust, Giant Hunt, Bloody Slash, Wild Strikes, Spinning Strikes, War Cry, and many more added since patch 1.07.

### 👁️ Foresight

The ability to read whether a given exchange is **winning or losing before it happens**, gained through experience. Determines whether to roll, block, or trade on reaction.

---

### 🪤 Frame Trap

A string of attacks that **cannot be escaped by rolling in place** — one hit forces a dodge, and subsequent hits roll-catch the dodge's recovery frames.

> [!NOTE]
> Frame traps can still be avoided by rolling **out of range** — rolling through them and staying in place is what fails.

**Examples:** Spinning Slash, Double Slash Follow-Up, light attack spam on Shamshir / Dagger / Thrusting Sword.

---

### 🎯 Free-Aiming

Manually aiming attacks (especially horizontal slashes) to alter their hitbox behaviour:

| Direction | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Up** | Prevents opponents from jumping over the attack |
| **Down** | Slightly extends range (e.g. Unsheathe R2, PS Great Spear CL1) |
| **Sideways (early)** | Hits on an earlier frame |
| **Sideways (late)** | Hits on a later frame — can enable true combos |
| **Backward** | Prevents opponent from whiff-punishing |

---

### 🧱 Fundamentals

Universal game skills required to master PvP with **any** weapon:

> Foresight · Spacing / Zoning · Reaction-Rolling · Game Knowledge · Stamina Management · Poise Counting

---

### 🗡️ Fundamentals Weapon

A weapon with **no gimmicks or ash-of-war dependency** — relies entirely on pure fundamentals and melee attacks. **Example:** PS Straight Swords.

| Name | Weapon |

## H

### 🔄 Hard-Swap vs. Soft-Swap

| Method | How | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| **Soft-swap** | Switch between equipped weapons | Single button press; plays a swap animation |
| **Hard-swap** | Replace weapon via inventory menu | No animation — weapon usable immediately after closing menu |

> [!NOTE]
> Hard-swapping is considered unfair by some players, but is **permitted** in all major ladder and tournament rulesets.

---

### ✝️ Holy Damage Bug

Holy damage is **not increased** by damage-boosting effects in PvP (e.g. Claw Talisman does not boost Coded Sword jump attacks in PvP, though it works fine in PvE). Any talisman or buff that directly increases damage simply has no effect on holy damage in PvP.

> **See also:** [Elden Ring PvP — Holy Damage Bug](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rIsP-EBdyfI) (YouTube)

---

### 🛡️ Hyper Armor

A state entered during specific attacks (common on heavy weapons like Greataxes) that grants **increased effective poise** and partially **recovers poise health** during the attack's duration.

> **See also:** [ER Poise Calculator](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/15gBgj4_QgHSbTfd7pc046PS4BzpA6NMCy3BGSs2Jd68/copy)

### 👻 i-Frame Duplication / Roll-Spam Immortality

Damage only lands if the opponent is **outside i-frames on both screens simultaneously**. With ~8 frames (270 ms) of latency + light/medium roll spam, an opponent can be permanently in i-frames on one screen or the other — making them immune to all damage except status effects.

> **See also:** [How Lag Makes You Invincible](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uPd9zpoi6eA) (YouTube)

---

### ❓ Irregular Poise Event (IPE)

Also called **Random Poise Event (RPE)**. Occasionally, an attack fails to stagger an opponent who clearly shouldn't be able to poise it — with **no flinch animation at all**. Most common when:

- A melee headshot occurs
- The opponent is turning as they are hit
- The opponent begins crouching as they are hit

The exact cause is unknown.

## K

### 🎯 Kill Confirmation

The act of finishing an opponent at low health **as reliably as possible** — typically with small, safe damage sources rather than risky commitments.

---

### 📚 Knowledge Check

A strategy or attack that **tests the opponent's game knowledge** — e.g. applying status effects to check if they know about resistance talismans or boluses.

> [!NOTE]
> Sometimes used condescendingly for strategies that become useless once the opponent learns about them.

## M

### 📐 Motion Value (MV)

Multipliers applied to weapon properties per attack, including:

- Physical / Affinity attack MVs
- Status buildup MVs
- Poise damage MVs
- Stamina damage MVs

A 1H R1 typically has **100 MV** — readable as a percentage of a base light attack.
Actual poise damage = `Poise Damage MV × Base Poise Damage of weapon`

> **See also:** [ER — Motion Values and Attack Data](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1j4bpTbsnp5Xsgw9TP2xv6d8R4qk0ErpE9r_5LGIDraU/edit#gid=0)

## N

### ⚖️ Neutral / Neutral Game

The state where **neither player has an advantage**.

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| ✅ **Advantage** | Pressuring the opponent |
| ❌ **Disadvantage** | Being pressured |
| ⚖️ **Neutral** | Neither player has the upper hand |

Players in neutral typically attempt to gain advantage through spacing, zoning, or movement.

---

### 🦘 Neutral Jump

Jumping **in place** (no directional input) to avoid an anticipated attack and counter with a jump attack. Functionally similar to a parry — a prediction — but generally safer and less precise.

> [!NOTE]
> Neutral jumps track well when performed while **walking**, but have **no tracking** during recovery frames (e.g. after an attack).

### ✨ Perfect Block

Blocking at the **exact same frame** as being hit — no block stun, but stamina is still lost. Appears to be a bug; the player sometimes takes full damage but completely bypasses hitstun.

---

### 👻 Phantom Headshot

A physical projectile (fan dagger, arrow, etc.) hitting the opponent's head triggers a long stagger animation — **even on a phantom hit**. No damage is dealt, but the stagger interrupts their roll on their own screen.

---

### 👻 Phantom Hit

You hit the opponent **on your screen**, but on their screen, they entered i-frames before the hit registered. Visual effects still play. No damage is dealt — but **status buildup applies normally**, potentially causing a phantom proc.

---

### 👻 Phantom Miss

The **opposite** of a phantom hit. You appear to get hit on your screen, but take no damage — because on the attacker's screen, you were still in i-frames or mid-strafe. Most noticeable when strafing projectiles or seemingly eating a roll-catch with no damage.

---

### 💀 Phantom Proc

If status buildup reaches 100% during a phantom hit, the **status effect still triggers** — but the damage component is skipped.

| Status | Normal Proc | Phantom Proc |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Blood Loss** | Damage + stun | Stun only |
| **Frost** | Damage + stun + debuff | Stun + debuff |
| **Poison** | DoT applied | DoT applied |
| **Scarlet Rot** | Rot applied | Rot applied |
| **Sleep** | Stun + FP drain | Stun + FP drain |
| **Madness** | Damage + FP drain + long stagger | FP drain + long stagger |
| **Death** | Death | Death |

---

### 🧱 Poise

An **invisible poise health bar** that depletes when hit (including phantom hits). Reaching zero causes a stagger.

| Event | Effect on Poise Health |
|-------|----------------------|
| Taking a hit | Decreases by the attack's poise damage |
| Entering hyper armor | Partially recovers (not necessarily to full) |
| 30 seconds without being hit | Resets to base poise value |

> [!NOTE]
> Poise values are fractional internally (e.g. Bullgoat armor gives ~99.6, displayed as 100). Poise damage is also fractional — always target **poise damage +1** to guarantee a break.

> **See also:** [Elden Ring Dissected #6 — Poise Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qeRVJOYQURM) (YouTube)

---

### 🧮 Poise Counting

Tracking your and your opponent's **current poise health** in real time. Knowing whether your next hit breaks their poise determines how aggressively you can play.

> [!WARNING]
> Avoid attacks that won't break your opponent's poise — they can poise trade right back through them.

---

### 🤺 Powerstance (PS)

Equipping two weapons of the **same class** (mainhand + offhand) to unlock paired **L1 attacks** using both weapons simultaneously.

> [!NOTE]
> "Powerstance" originates from Dark Souls 2 — it's not used in Elden Ring itself. In Elden Ring, *"Paired Weapons"* refers specifically to weapons that grant a PS moveset when two-handed (e.g. Ornamental Straight Sword).

---

### ⚡ Priority

In an exchange, one player often has **priority** — if both attack simultaneously, that player wins the trade. Priority comes from:

- Faster attack startup
- Higher poise damage (breaks opponent's hyper armor)
- Being outside the opponent's counter-frames (they're still staggered)

Without priority, the correct response is to **reset to neutral**, create distance, or attempt a parry / neutral jump.

> [!NOTE]
> Rolling and crouching attacks are the **same attack** in Elden Ring — CR1 is unambiguous.

---

### 🔮 Read / Hard Read

A **read** is predicting an opponent's action and pre-emptively punishing it.

A **hard read** is a high-commitment prediction — e.g. a parry or neutral jump — where significant punishment follows if the read is wrong.

---

### 🔄 Recovery Cancel

Moves have recovery frames before returning to neutral. Many can be shortened by performing specific actions (e.g. crouching cancels the long UGS crouch poke recovery).

> **See also:** [Elden Ring Frame Data Explorer](https://er-frame-data.nyasu.business/)

---

### 🍝 Reverse Backstep (Ravioli)

Queue a backstep during attack recovery, then **walk backward** — the backstep travels **forward** (toward the opponent) rather than away. Follow with a backstep attack.

| Animation | Startup | To Attack | To AoW | To Roll |
|-----------|:-------:|:---------:|:------:|:-------:|
| Medium Backstep | 2 | 9–11 | 12–14 | 16–18 |

*Recovery windows depend on equipment load.*

Faster than recovering into a running attack, and closes distance aggressively.

---

### 🔁 Reverse Quickstep

Lock on → aim backward → quickstep + unlock simultaneously. The quickstep travels **toward the locked target** despite the backward input. Has i-frames — safer for backstabbing than Reverse Backstep.

> **See also:** [The Reverse Quickstep | Elden Ring](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BIkFlcnGJuQ) (YouTube)

---

### 🪤 Roll-Catching Combo

A series of attacks the opponent **can** escape with well-timed rolls, but **cannot** escape with panic rolls (button mashing immediately on hit).

| Example |
|---------|
| Great Spear R1 → R2 |
| Great Spear RR1 → R1 |
| Halberd RR1 → Impaling Thrust |
| Halberd RR1 → Flaming Strike |
| Unsheathe R1/R2 → Unsheathe R1 |

> [!NOTE]
> No precise timing required — simply queuing the next attack is sufficient.

### 📈 Rune Level (RL)

The character's level.

| RL | Usage |
|----|-------|
| **125** | Primary competitive / ladder meta |
| **150** | Secondary popular level |
| **137** | Invasion-optimized — invades both 125 and 150 ranges |

### 🦘 Rising / Falling / Landing Jump Attack

| Type | When | Tracking |
|------|------|:--------:|
| **Rising** | Performed early (gaining height) | Poor |
| **Falling** | Performed late (losing height) | Strong |
| **Landing** | Character touches ground during the attack | Strong |

Falling and landing jump attacks can track targets even when the jump was aimed away from them.

Performing a light attack **while actively blocking**. The attacker can be guard-broken during this.

### 📉 Soft Cap vs. Hard Cap

A **soft cap** is a stat value after which returns significantly diminish. The **hard cap** is the absolute maximum (99).

| Stat | Primary Soft Caps | Notes |
|------|:-----------------:|-------|
| **VIG** | 40, 60 | HP; also 40/60 Immunity, 30/40/60 Fire Defense |
| **MIN** | 15, 35, 60 | FP; also 40/60 Focus |
| **END** | 15, 30, 50 / 60 | Stamina / Carry Capacity; 30/40/60 Robustness |
| **STR** | 20, 55, 80 | AR; 30/40/60 Physical Defense |
| **DEX** | 20, 55, 80 | AR; also 15/30/45 Incantation Scaling |
| **INT** | 20, 50, 80 / 60, 80 | AR / Sorcery Scaling; 20/35/60 Magic Defense |
| **FTH** | 20, 50, 80 / 60, 80 | AR / Incantation Scaling |
| **ARC** | 20 / ~50 / 80 | AR; 25/45/60 melee status; 15/40/60 Vitality; 20/35/60 Holy Defense |

*Actual scaling depends on the weapon and its infusion — these are approximations.*

---

### 📏 Spacing / Spacing Weapon

**Spacing** is positioning yourself just outside an attack's range to bait a whiff, then punishing the recovery. Refers to micro-positioning around **individual attacks**.

A **spacing weapon** (e.g. PS Straight Swords) is primarily played by spacing out and punishing whiffs, or gaining priority up close.

*Compare with **Zoning** — spacing is micro (per attack), zoning is macro (overall neutral positioning).*

---

### ⚗️ Split Damage vs. Pure Damage

| Type | Description | Competitive Value |
|------|-------------|:-----------------:|
| **Pure Damage** | All damage of a single type (physical, holy, etc.) | ✅ Stronger — one defence calculation |
| **Split Damage** | Damage split between physical + affinity | ⚠️ Weaker — each portion has its own calculation |

Higher AR vs. one defence stat produces better scaling than moderate AR split across two.

---

### 🛡️ Stamina Damage

Damage dealt to a blocking opponent's stamina bar.

**Formula:** `BaseStDmg × UpgrMod × StDmgMV/100 × (GF − GBoost×AF/100/GF) × PvPMod`

| Modifier | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **UpgrMod** | 1.0 at +0 → 2.0 at +25 |
| **PvPMod (melee)** | 1.325 |
| **PvPMod (ashes/spells)** | 1.25 |
| **GF (Barricade Shield)** | 0.5 |
| **GF (Greatshield Talisman)** | 0.8 |

**Notable stamina damage sources:**

| Move | St. Dmg MV |
|------|:----------:|
| Kick | 999 |
| Square Off R1 (Broadsword) | 700 |
| Unsheathe R2 | 300 |
| PS Great Spear JL1 | 300 |
| Great Spear R2 (full charge) | 265 |

### ✂️ Stance Cancelling

Also called **Ash of War Stagger Cancelling**. When hit from behind by a **Damage Level 3 + attack direction 0** (frontal thrust) attack — e.g. a UGS crouch poke — using a "stance" ash of war (e.g. Unsheathe) lets you enter i-frames **far earlier** than normal stagger recovery, escaping vortex setups.

> **See also:** [New TECH! Escape ANY VORTEX!](https://youtu.be/DC9lOUyMdag) (YouTube)

## T

### ⏱️ Time to Kill (TTK)

How long it takes to kill an opponent. Elden Ring at higher RL has a **low TTK** — many colossal swords can eliminate a player in ~4 crouch pokes, and some ashes of war deal 1000+ damage (roughly half a player's total HP).

---

### ⚔️ Trade / Trading

When both players hit each other simultaneously.

| Trade Type | Cause | How to Avoid |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| **Perfect Trade** | Both active frames connect at the exact same moment | Fundamentals, luck |
| **Poise Trade** | One/both players hyper-poise through the hit | High poise damage weapons (e.g. UGS) |
| **Hyperarmor Trade** | Poise trade caused specifically by hyper armor | Fast + high-damage weapons (e.g. 2H Halberd) |
| **Latency Trade** | Lag allows an attack to land despite being hit first | Low recovery weapons; rolling after each attack |
| **Ranged Trade** | Both players fire projectiles simultaneously | Don't fight ranged vs. ranged |
| **Shield Poke Trade** | One player pokes through the opponent's attack | High range weapons, jump attacks |

---

### 🔗 True Combo

A sequence of attacks the opponent **cannot escape by rolling** (poise is separate — sufficient poise can still break out of some combos).

| Example |
|---------|
| 2H Heavy Thrusting Sword JR2 → R1 |
| 2H Heavy Thrusting Sword JR1 → R1 |
| 1H Guardian's Swordspear JR2 → R1 |
| Transient Moonlight R1 → R1 (or → Carian Slicer) |
| 1H Colossal Sword CR1 → offhand weapon |
| Storm Stomp → R1 (most weapons) |

> [!NOTE]
> Some true combos can be escaped via Quickstep or other WAs with immediate i-frames. Some require specific weapon angles. Opponents with enough poise may also break free.

## V

### 🌀 Unsheathe Vortex / Moonveil Vortex

Unsheathe (and Transient Moonlight / Moonveil skill) ends with long recovery, cancelable by rolling / jumping / crouching / blocking.

**Vortex setup:** Cancel into crouch → hold L2 to re-sheathe → Unsheathe again at high speed.
Unsheathe → Unsheathe is a roll-catching combo, and at point blank it's very difficult to reaction-roll consistently.

---

### ❤️ Vigor Check

When a single hit empties an opponent's HP bar, revealing exactly how little health — and therefore how little Vigor — they had. A humorous term for punishing low-Vigor builds.

> [!NOTE]
> At RL 125, a player should typically have **60 Vigor** or very close to it.

---

### 🌀 Vortex

A state where the player is **stuck in disadvantage** — any escape attempt perpetuates the same trapped state.

> **Classic example:** Great Spear CR1 → opponent staggers → follow-up poke before recovery → forced panic roll → Great Spear roll-catches the panic roll → repeat.

**Common vortex examples:**

| Vortex | Core Loop |
|--------|-----------|
| **Great Spear** | CR1 → stagger → follow-up poke → forced panic roll → roll catch |
| **Halberd + Offstoc** | Stagger with poke → offhand stab confirmed |
| **Unsheathe / Moonveil** | Unsheathe → cancel → Unsheathe → roll catch |
| **PS Great Hammers / Greataxes** | Hyper armor trades + knockdowns |

---

### 🏄 Wavedashing / Goblin Step

Repeatedly crouching and uncrouching while running. For a brief window after uncrouching, a **crouch attack can still be performed mid-run**.

> **See also:** [Elden Ring — WAVEDASH Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R2-f9pz5t5Y) (YouTube)

---

### 🔮 Weapon Art

The **Dark Souls 3 term** for weapon skills. In Elden Ring:
- **Skill** — what it's called when used in combat
- **Ash of War** — the equippable item that adds a skill to a weapon

Both terms are used interchangeably in the community.

---

### 💨 Whiff / Whiff-Punish

**Whiff** — an attack misses due to improper spacing (too far, or opponent moved out of range).

**Whiff-punish** — attacking the opponent during their whiff recovery.

---

### 🗺️ Zoning

A macro-level positional strategy — using projectiles, long-ranged attacks, and fast safe moves to **control space** in neutral and force the opponent into disadvantage.

Heavy zoning: Sorcery builds, Light Bow.

*Compare with **Spacing** — spacing is micro (per attack), zoning is macro (overall neutral positioning).*
