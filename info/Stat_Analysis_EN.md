## 📖 Introduction & Methodology

Stat breakpoints are not always intuitive. Many people think 40 and 60 are the default diminishing returns points in all souls-like games, but the data is more complex than that. For instance, in Dark Souls 3 the Vigor breakpoints were 17/26/44.

Elden Ring handles some stats a little oddly — often the first points you invest give very little, but each additional point gives more and more up to a cutoff. This means you generally want a stat either **as low as possible** or **directly at the breakpoint**, with anything in-between costing you value. This is why classes with the lowest starting stats in things you don't want are most valuable for min/maxing.

**Data collection method:** Started as a level 1 Wretch with 10 in all stats, leveled one stat at a time to 99 while hand-recording each change. Character was completely unequipped to avoid unwanted contributions. Casting power was tested using the Grace at Warmaster's Shack, attacking the nearby wolves with Flame Sling (Faith), Glintstone Pebble (Intelligence), and similar spells.

> [!NOTE]
> The **hard cap** for all stats is **99**.
> Weapon upgrade level (e.g. +25) does **not** change breakpoint positions — it only multiplies the scaling bonuses.

## 📈 Stat Breakpoints

### ❤️ Vigor — 40 / 58

> *"Attribute governing HP. If reduced to nothing, you die. Also affects fire resistance and immunity — your resistance to various poisons and rot."*

| Range | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 → 40 | Strong HP gains, ramp up toward 40 |
| 40 → 58 | Noticeable decrease in efficiency |
| 58 → 60 | Very little benefit |

> [!TIP]
> HP gains ramp up heavily toward 40 — always take Vigor to 40. Going to 58 is situational and generally not recommended.

---

### 💧 Mind — 40 / 50

> *"Attribute that determines FP. Also affects focus — your resistance to sleep and madness."*

| Range | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 → 41 | FP continues to ramp up |
| 41 → 50 | Diminishing returns begin |
| 40 | Focus resistance hard decrease |

> [!TIP]
> If you use FP, take Mind to 50. At minimum take it to 40. If you want exactly as much FP as a max upgraded Flask restores, stop at **38**.

---

### 🏃 Endurance — 25 / 40 / 50 / 60

> *"Attribute governing stamina and equip load. Also affects robustness — your resistance to blood loss and frostbite."*

| Breakpoint | What stops growing |
|------------|-------------------|
| 25 | Equip Load (first stop) |
| 40 | Robustness hard decrease |
| 50 | Stamina hard stop |
| 60 | Equip Load (final stop) |

> [!TIP]
> If you primarily cast spells, leave Endurance at 25. Otherwise take it to at least 30. For melee builds, 50 is a strong target for stamina.

---

### 💪 Strength — 20 / ~53 / 80 *(2H: 35 / 53)*

> *"Attribute boosting attack power of strength-scaling weapons and affecting physical defense."*

| Range | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 → 20 | Heavily front-loaded, largest gains |
| 20 → ~53 | Slower growth (fuzzy range of 50–53) |
| 53 → 80 | Diminishing returns |
| 60+ | Physical defense efficiency drops |

> [!NOTE]
> **2-Handing** a weapon multiplies your effective Strength by **1.5x**, so stopping at **35** gives an effective value of **52.5**.

> [!TIP]
> Take Strength to 20 first, then focus on survivability. Later push to 35 (2H) or 53. Going to 80 is not recommended unless 2-Handing.

---

### ⚡ Dexterity — 20 / ~53 / 80

> *"Attribute boosting attack power of dexterity-scaling weapons, reducing casting time, softening fall damage, and making it harder to be knocked off your horse."*

| Range | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 → 20 | Heavily front-loaded |
| 20 → ~53 | Slower growth |
| 53+ | Steep decline after 80 |

> [!TIP]
> Take Dexterity to 20 first, then focus on survivability before pushing to 53. Going to 80 is generally not worthwhile.

---

### 🔮 Intelligence

> *"Attribute boosting magic power of intelligence-scaling sorceries and improving magic resistance."*

| Type | Breakpoints |
|------|-------------|
| **Attack Power** | 20 / 50 / 80 |
| **Casting Damage** | 30 / 45 / 60 / 80 *(varies by staff)* |

| Catalyst Type | Breakpoints |
|---------------|-------------|
| Multi-stat staves | 30 / 45 |
| Pure Intelligence staves | 60 / 80 |
| Prince of Death's Staff *(exception)* | 60 / 80 hybrid |

> [!NOTE]
> Magic resistance has a hard decrease at 20.

> [!TIP]
> Take Intelligence to 20 first, then focus on survivability. If committing to a casting build, go all the way to 50 or 80.

---

### 🙏 Faith

> *"Attribute boosting magic power of faith-scaling incantations."*

| Type | Breakpoints |
|------|-------------|
| **Attack Power** | 20 / 50 / 80 |
| **Casting Damage** | 30 / 45 / 60 / 80 *(varies by seal)* |

| Catalyst Type | Breakpoints |
|---------------|-------------|
| Multi-stat seals | 30 / 45 |
| Pure Faith seals | 60 / 80 |

> [!TIP]
> Take Faith to 20 first, then focus on survivability. If committing to a casting build, go all the way to 50 or 80.

---

### 🐉 Arcane — 20 / 45

> *"Attribute governing discovery. Also affects holy defense, certain sorceries and incantations, and vitality — your resistance to instant death."*

| Type | Breakpoints |
|------|-------------|
| **Attack Power** | 20 / ~50 / 80 |
| **Casting Damage** | 30 / 45 *(Albinauric Staff, Dragon Communion Seal)* |

| Stat | Breakpoint |
|------|------------|
| Holy Defense | 20 |
| Vitality (instant death resist) | 15 / 39 |
| Bleed buildup | Plateaus around 44–46 |
| Discovery | Near-linear up to 99 |

> [!NOTE]
> Arcane has **no pure catalyst** — only hybrid ones, so casting breakpoints are always 30/45.

> [!TIP]
> Take Arcane to 20 first, then focus on survivability before pushing to ~45. Going to 80 is generally not worthwhile.

## 🗺️ Example Leveling Path

1. Pick **two attack stats** (e.g. STR/FTH, DEX/INT, STR/DEX) and push both to **20** as soon as possible
2. Branch out — push Endurance to **25**, then Vigor and/or Mind to **40**
3. From there, go deeper into an attack stat or raise Endurance further (50 for melee, 80 for casting)
4. To limit FP to exactly what a max upgraded Flask restores → stop Mind at **38**
5. If 2-Handing, stopping Strength at **35** saves levels (equals 52.5 effective)

## 💾 Bonus: How to Transfer Saves Between Accounts

> [!CAUTION]
> This only copies the save in **slot #1**. Multiple slots require additional steps not covered here.
> **Always back up your save file before proceeding.**

**Requirements:** A hex editor such as [HxD](https://mh-nexus.de/en/hxd/). Find your Steam ID at [steamid.io](https://steamid.io/).

**Save file location:** `%appdata%/EldenRing/[YourSteamID]/ER0000.sl2`

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Open the **donor's** save file in HxD |
| 2 | Right-click → **Select Block** → Start: `310`, End: `28030F` (Hex) |
| 3 | Right-click → **Copy** |
| 4 | Open **your** save file → Select the same block (`310` to `28030F`) |
| 5 | Right-click → **Paste Write** |
| 6 | **Search → Replace**: find donor's SteamID, replace with yours. Set Datatype to **Integer number**, Direction: **All** |
| 7 | Select block `310` to `28030F` again |
| 8 | **Analysis → Checksums → MD-5** → copy the generated checksum |
| 9 | Select block `300` to `30F` → **Paste Write** the checksum |
| 10 | Save the file and delete `ER0000.sl2.bak` from your SteamID folder |

> [!TIP]
> If this seems complex, look for **EldenRingSaveCopier** — an automated tool that handles this process.
