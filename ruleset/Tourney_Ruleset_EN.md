## 📌 Priorities in Rules

### ⚖️ Balancing between skilful gameplay and setup variety

Different rules' powerlevels have different levels of skilful gameplay and setup variety so that people have more fun. This is the main reason why we have many of the top setups banned, as the majority of them do not have as much skill expression (like thrusting shields) and the ruleset would become dominated by any single one of them that would be unbanned leaving little room for alternatives. This is also why some setups have damage restrictions rather than outright banning them, as this increases the pool of viable setups. This ruleset isn't the only way to do it and we could definitely move the power-level slider in either direction.

### 📋 Ruleset Bloat

Whilst we could balance every single aspect of the game to make it as perfectly balanced as possible, the ruleset would become extremely unwieldy even compared to the current ruleset. That is why there are occasionally rules, like the player buff ban, that have a few unnecessary bans tied to it in order to lump together all of the problematic things that need to be banned under one rule.

### 🗳️ Community consensus

Even if not everyone can be happy about a rules change/decision, we want to prioritise ensuring that the community feels heard and that they have a say in the ruleset that they're playing in. The community votes on rules and rarely is a change put in place that hasn't already gotten a substantial amount of votes.

### ⏱️ Anti-Stall

These rulesets have been designed for the sake of being used in Tournaments and Ladder. Especially for tournaments that are streamed, in the interest of time there's a large amount of stall tactics, or buffs that force the opponent to stall which we have banned in order to ensure that the tournaments finish efficiently. Additionally, stalling tactics are generally not fun for either player, so these tactics are also banned for the interest of keeping the game fun and interactive for everyone involved.

### ⏳ Increasing TTK

TTK, meaning Time To Kill, is referenced a lot in rules discussions because one of our priorities is that we want to make sure that fights take a meaningful amount of interactions to showcase a difference in skill. We increase the TTK in order to increase the sample size of decision making and interactions so that on average the better player will come out on top more often with a fight.

### 🏆 Competitive Integrity

Whilst not a hard requirement, something we want is for the ruleset to promote active competition between players, so *some* strategies that are cheesy, or one sided in terms of effort may be limited or banned in order to ensure that we keep a level of Competitive Integrity between players. Part of this is due to tournaments being some of the most public showings of how the ruleset functions, and so we want less opportunities for players to be pushed away from participating.

### 🤝 Fairness

While not all setups are created equally, there's still a goal to ensure that players don't have a completely mismatched amount of effort needed to win. For example, with Stone Club — if both players play completely perfectly, Stone Club should never be able to win. However, people aren't robots and the room for mistakes when fighting against a setup like that is vastly smaller than the amount of mistakes the Stone Club player can get away with, which explains its restrictions.

## 🚫 General Rules

### Player Buffs Globally Banned

Any buff that stays for its duration regardless of equipped weapon.

> [!NOTE]
> Excludes effects from crystal tears / talismans / equipment / armour.

The counterplay for the majority of buffs in this game is to either mirror your opponent or stall it out which is something that we want to avoid as part of the ruleset's priorities. These buffs may include things such as Black Flame Protection, Radiant Baldachin's Blessing, Unseen Form, Rallying Standard, Starlight Shards, etc.

### All Passive HP Regen Banned

- All Passive tick-based HP Regen via Armour / Talismans / Shields / Ashes
- All Methods of Passive Regen / Flat Heals / % Heals applied via Spells, Crystal Tears and Consumables

> [!NOTE]
> **Exceptions** — Sources that apply a manually triggerable heal are exempt.
> Godskin Swaddling Cloth, Blood Tax, Prayerful Strike, Greatstars, etc.
> These require attacking your opponent, promoting interactive gameplay rather than stalling.

### Rune Arc — Allowed in Better Multiplayer / DEN-Maps only

If in Better Multiplayer / Den Maps, all players can use rune arcs. If playing on vanilla PC or console, only the world's host can use a rune arc, which is not a fair duel. When all players can use a rune arc it offers unique build-design opportunities and a better HP pool overall.

### Blue Flasks limited to 2

Certain mage strategies are able to consistently loop spells that set up unavoidable damage and can set up completely safe blue flasks. With the 2 flask limit, those strategies become less viable due to the FP cost that they impose, forcing mages to focus on being more precise with their gameplan.

For example, **Founding Rain of Stars** creates a large zone where the opponent is constantly being damaged, adds visual clutter, and causes aimpunch making players extremely disincentivised from pressuring into it. Meanwhile, frametrap spells such as **Star Shower** keep chipping the opponent outside the zone. These two spells can be safely looped for every blue flask the user has. This cheese strategy was dealt with by limiting blue flasks rather than banning any spells directly, as they are very FP intensive.

### Additional Global Bans

| Item | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Fan Daggers** | Unavoidable and unpunishable ranged chip damage. If legal, both players would use all 40 every single fight |
| **Spectral Steed Whistle (Torrent)** | Has nothing to do with duelling. Feel free to host your own jousting tournaments |
| **Spirit Ashes** | Has nothing to do with duelling. Feel free to host your own Pokémon tournaments |
| **Blocking with Shields** | Dagshield hard counters the majority of setups. Viable counter-setups are limited to sham/estoc, and even in those matchups shields shut down mix-ups and make fights extremely passive |

## 📜 General Rulings

### Intended Use

For banned content, **equipping them is treated as intended use** and results in a round loss.

> [!NOTE]
> Equipping a banned AoW but not using it is an exception.

This draws a hard line on rulebreaks to avoid wasting time during tourneys discussing whether a violation was "on purpose." The responsibility is on the players to not accidentally break a rule.

### 3 Ash Ruleset

Not currently a ladder rule, but used in several tournaments. This ruleset legalises all ashes of wars, but **players may only use an ash of war 3 separate times per fight**.

> [!NOTE]
> Ash of war followups do not add to the count.
> Repeated presses of Stormblade / Weedcutter / Flamestrike followups all count as one usage.

Generally well-balanced since spamming ashes cannot dominate the fight and there's an opportunity cost to choosing one broken ash over another.

## 🧪 Crystal Tears

| Banned Tear | Summary |
|-------------|---------|
| **Deflecting Hardtear** | Deflects + guard counters allow any slow setup to be completely hard countered |
| **Leaden Hardtear** | 10 seconds of stun immunity. Exists to set up cheese strategies with no counterplay |
| **Winged Crystal Tear** | Allows light rolling with the heaviest setups, bypassing the light load ban and enabling endurance-free damage builds |
| **Windy Crystal Tear** | Boosts dodge I-frames. Leads to too many mistakes going unpunished, especially on higher latencies |
| **Stonebarb Cracked Tear** | No opportunity cost, causes several setups to overperform, and heavily incentivises stalling to counter it |
| **All Stat Boosting Crystal Tears** | Effectively provides a level 135 build equivalent. Primarily benefits already meta setups |
| **All Damage Boosting Crystal Tears** | Banned to reduce player damage output and maintain higher TTK |

## 🛡️ Equipment

### Light Load (<30% Roll) Banned

Despite being forced onto weaker armour, light roll consistently outperforms medium load by rolling outside the range of most weapon attacks, making rollcatches impossible. In a game where mixups setting up rollcatches are so important, this consistently undermines competitive integrity as mistakes cannot be punished.

### Banned Armour

| Armour | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| **All Pieces of Briar Armour** | Adds damage on rolls but banned due to the aimpunch bug — attack hitboxes get flicked out of position when poising, causing correctly timed attacks to whiff randomly |
| **All Pieces of Rakshasa's Armour** | 2% damage buff per piece, optimally swapped to at low HP where defences no longer matter. Banned alongside Red Feather to reduce low-HP damage output |

## 💎 Talismans

| **Spear Talisman (Sword Lance only)** | Sword Lance's already high base damage and ability to force trades means spear talisman pushes it to overperform |
| **Red Feather Talisman** | Too much of a damage boost at low HP. Allows a losing player to skip the opponent's Blue Feather range entirely and close out the fight in far fewer interactions, creating too much variance |

## 🧴 Greases

| Banned Item | Reason |
|-------------|--------|
| **Rot Greases** | The reward for inflicting rot is so high it allows players to snowball pressure way too easily |
| **Sleep Greases on Ripple Weapons** | Built-in S Arcane scaling causes sleep buildup to scale from grease usage, allowing sleep in very few hits — including ghost hits |
| **Poison Greases on 2H Shamshir / Scimitar** | Fast moveset inflicts poison very quickly. High damage bolus punish via jumping R2 R1 true combo. Scimitar is banned for the same reasons |
| **All Greases on 2H Twinblades** | Long considered to deal too much damage without restrictions. Grease restriction is chosen over buff restriction due to Twinblades' reliance on Spinning Slash creating a tangible opportunity cost for self-buff AoWs |

## ⚔️ Buff Restrictions

Limiting ALL buffs — not just greases — prevents players from circumventing grease restrictions with AoW or spell buffs. These bans are based on the current meta's power level and are subject to change as the meta develops.

> [!WARNING]
> All Greases, Spells, and Ashes of War that apply a buff to the weapon are **banned** on the following weapons.

| Weapon | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| **2H Thrusting Sword** | Still a top-3 setup without any buffs in the current meta |
| **2H Stone Club / Club** | Small weapon speed with great weapon hyperarmour and damage. Also serves as a status restriction |
| **2H Sword Lance** | High base damage + ability to force trades means damage buffs push it to overperform |
| **2H Raptor's Talons** | Highest damage infusion (Lightning) banned alongside buff restriction to keep it in check |
| **Offhand Dagger** | Fully unreactable, frametrap backswings, very low recovery. Flat damage buffs make it deal too much damage |
| **Offhand Axe** | High base damage, borderline unreactable, poisebreaks and frametraps on backswing. Already used on the strongest setups |
| **Mainhand 1H Wakizashi** | Unreactable light attacks and hard priority on the first two heavies. Used in a huge variety of setups |
| **2H Greatspears** | Extremely long-ranged unreactable attacks and hyperarmour for high damage vortexes |
| **2H Star Fists** | High damage and unreactable attacks. Buff restriction pushes players toward the slight downgrade of Iron Balls |

## 💨 Ashes of War (AoW)

> [!WARNING]
> All of the following AoWs are extremely spammable and low-risk relative to their reward. If used in a duel, they affect almost every single interaction throughout the fight.

### 🔴 Evasion AoWs

| AoW | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| **Bloodhound's Step (BHS)** | Frame 1 I-frames on *press* (roll is on *release*). Classified as a "fast AoW" enabling quicker recovery cancels into I-frames. Its range also causes attacks that would track a normal roll to fail |
| **Quickstep** | Same reasons as BHS — Frame 1 I-frames on press, fast AoW classification for quicker recovery cancels |
| **Raptor of the Mists** | Same reasons as BHS. Can also be cancelled into a crouch then re-used, easily overlapping I-frames on higher latencies |
| **Blind Spot** | Safe I-frame attack that cancels recoveries similarly to BHS/QS. The range of the attack makes whiff punishing it completely unrealistic |

### 🔴 Dominant Performance AoWs

| AoW | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| **Flaming Strike** | Lingering hitbox makes it practically unpunishable in neutral. Level 2 hitstun always sets up trade combos. Low FP cost allows dozens of safe uses per duel. Also one of the easiest roll catches due to its frametrap and high damage follow-up |
| **Swift Slash** | Always guarantees damage via frametraps if the startup isn't interrupted. Extremely high damage when the full ash lands |
| **Storm Stomp** | Fast hyperarmour to take winning trades on whiff punish attempts, lingering hitbox makes it hard to punish when whiffed, and doubles as an effective rollcatch tool |
| **Storm Assault, Stormcaller & Red Bear Hunt** | Very fast hyperarmour startup, high damage for winning trades, and hard to punish due to lingering hitboxes |
| **Barbaric Roar** | Converts the heavy attack chain into a fast, high-damage attack that poises through colossal weapon attacks. Most effective on Star Fists where attempting to poise through it results in poise chip looping |
| **Needle Piercer** | Removes all buffs on target even on ghost hits. The ruleset's reliance on crystal tears to manage TTK makes this too warping |
| **Painful Strikes (Tooth Whip AoW)** | Once poise chip is set up, it becomes a dagger-speed high-damage ash that true combos into the offhand. Can loop poise chip via backswing with an offhand axe |

### 🔴 Buff / Damage AoWs

| AoW | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| **All Parries** | Extremely low recovery makes whiff punishing them possible only for frame advantage. Many setup parries simply require reading a trade situation and are rewarded with near-instakills. TTK increases have made parries even more effective |
| **Endure** | Defence and poise increase essentially negates all pressure, forcing opponents to back off. Shuts down many avenues of skill expression |
| **Oath of Vengeance** | Same issues as Endure, but lasts longer and raises all stats by 5 for 60 seconds |
| **Royal Knights Resolve** | 40% damage buff for next hit. Low recovery makes reapplication trivial — effectively no downside |
| **Determination** | 30% damage buff for next hit. Same issue as Royal Knights Resolve |
| **Shriek of Sorrow** | Scales up to 20% damage boost at low HP, effectively acting as a second Red Feather Talisman |

### 🔴 Chill-based AoW

| AoW | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| **Chilling Mist on Cold Infused Weapons** | Sets up a 3-hit cold proc applying a 20% defence debuff, then swaps to a higher damage infusion. Counterplay boils down to stalling or mirroring |

### 🔴 Animation-based AoW

| AoW | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| **Fast Sword Dance (on setups using the fast animation)** | Fast hyperarmour startup, good damage, hard to roll consistently. Note: Shamshir uses the fast sword dance animation |

## 🐛 Blacklisted Glitches / Techs

> [!CAUTION]
> Some of these may be used accidentally — please make sure to read carefully.

| Glitch / Tech | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Armor swap poise reset** | Removing armour until poise drops below the amount chipped, then re-equipping, resets poise health to 100% without attacking. Removes a lot of setup variety by killing setups that rely on poise counting |
| **Block Desync** | The main method of setting up a desync, making attacks completely unanimated. Banned for the obvious reason of consistently replicable attacks with no animation |
| **Offhand drawstring poison grease** | Using poison grease on a weapon being two-handed via the offhand causes much higher poison buildup on multi-hitting attacks. Poison grease is banned on offhand weapons in general to avoid this bug |
